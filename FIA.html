FIA Rulebook

v3.3.2 - 22.08.23

Section 1:Pre-Lobby Setup

Section 1A- If a lobby is setup incorrectly, then the race results will be invalidated and treated as though it never happened.

Section 1B- Races for each tier will be held at the following times of 20:00 GMT on Sunday for Tier 1, Monday for tier 2.

Section 1C- Lobby host will be responsible for ensuring the event is setup correctly.

Section 1D- Event will consist of a short qualifying session and a 50% race except when explicitly stated.

Section 1E- Damage will be set to Standard/Standard.

Section 1F- Safety car will be set to Reduced.

Section 1G- Red Flag will be set to Standard.

Section 1H- Cars will do a formation lap.

Section 1I- Manual race starts must be turned on.

Section 1J- Drivers will have full control over car setups.

Section 1K- Corner cutting will be set to strict corner cutting.

Section 1L- All drivers should choose their correct car when entering the lobby.

Section 1M- Spectator spots reserved for commentators

Section 1N- Brake assists will be limited to low and additionally pit assists will be disabled.

Section 1O- Ghosting must be turned on for Tier 1 and Tier 2.

Section 1P- Parc Ferme regulations will apply.

Section 2:Qualifying

Section 2A- Driver must pay attention to the other drivers to ensure that they don’t block other laps to the best of their ability.

Section 2B- Intentionally slowing down a car on an outlap will be treated like hot lap blocking and will result in similar consequence.

Section 2C- Standard penalties for blocking a Hotlap during Qualifying is a Qualification Ban and subsequently 2 Penalty Points.

Section 2D- The FIA will have the final verdict relating to qualifying incidents.

Section 2E- Drivers have right to an appeal.

Section 2F- Retiring on track during any session is punishable, and will grant 2 penalty points.

Section 2G- AI are treated like a normal driver. If somebody’s AI blocks a flying lap then it will be punished Identically to if the driver had done it.

Section 2K- Resetting to track is illegal and will result in 2 penalty points as standard and may see harsher penalties handed out in circumstances where disruption is caused.

Section 2L- Drivers must take every corner, as they would in a normal lap, on an outlap.

Section 2M- Drivers cutting corners on their outlap may be awarded a qualifying ban.

Section 2N- Driver serving a qualifying ban may not cross the line to start their lap. The consequence will be the ban will carry over.

Section 2O- The ban can carry over sessions when not served in a correct manner.

Section 3:Session Start & Formation Lap

Section 3A- Each event will have a ready up time announced (BST), and any driver which starts the session prematurely will be given 2 Penalty Points, as this effects other drivers preperation time.

Section 3B- 10 Car lengths maximum must be left between 2 cars on a formation lap.

Section 3C- Drivers are expected to drive safely as they would in a race.

Section 3D- Collisions made with another cars will result in a 10 second time penalty and 2 penalty points. This penalty is harsh as there should be absolutely no contact during a Formation Lap, and doing so heavily impacts race starts for affected drivers.

Section 4:Racing

Section 4A- Drivers are expected to have good racing standards throughout the race.

Section 4B- Failling below the reasonable racing standards may result in a penalty

Section 4C- The FIA will investigate any poor racing standard below reasonable, providing video footage is sent.

Section 4D- Video footage should be clear and the offence clearly visible.

Section 4E- Penalties (And Warning) for using runoff will be removed provided the following conditions are met:
It was necessary to use the runoff;
The driver reduces their speed or makes a reasonable effort to do so;
The delta gained, if any, is sufficiently handed back;
Video provided to the FIA clearly showing the race director screen and the incident in its entirety.

Section 4F- Drivers will be discretionarily punished for forcing another car off track, Provided the above conditions are met:
The car forced off track holds a valid claim to the corner;
The car forced off track holds a honest belief that the corner could be fought through safely;
The car forced off track can provide video footage that clearly shows points A and B being met;
The car forcing the other car off track didn’t take every precaution necessary to avoid the incident.

Section 4G- Drivers should take every effort possible to enter the pit lane safely, avoiding situations such as:
Cutting the racing line to enter;
Dummying after having all tyers over the pit entry line;
Cutting the solid white lines

Section 4H- Resetting to track rules are the same as stated in Section 2H. In racing situations, the driver will be awarded a 10 second time penalty and 2 penalty points when caught Resetting on track.

Section 4I- All other cases of resetting to track are illegal however the FIA will consider necessary defenses.

Section 4J- All drivers wanting to retire from the race should do so in the pits or otherwise risk punishment.

Section 4K- An intentional retirement, for example crashing out on purpose, will result in an internal review and very heavy consequences if found guilty which could exceed race bans.

Section 4L- When returning to the track drivers should do so safely.

Section 4M- Dangerous Re-Entry consists of:
They re-join on the apex of the corner forcing passing cars to take evasive action;
The joining car provided collides with the passing car;
The off-Track car illegally resets to track.

Section 4N- Drivers are not entitled to space when:
Putting their car in a non-Existent space;
The move would force the car to spin in an ordinary condition with a reasonably competent driver.

Section 4O- Drivers moving under braking may be punished if any off the below are satisfied:
Contact was made resulting in damage to either car;
The attacking car was forced off track;
Resulting contact causes both drivers to lose out to a third party.

Section 4P- Weaving on the straight is only acceptable when there is sufficient space between yourself and any car either behind or in front of you for warming tyres only. It is illegal to weave on the straight to defend, if caught moving more than 3 times on a straight, it will result in a 5 second time penalty and 2 penalty points.

Section 4Q- Drivers may only make one movement into a breaking zone and must not move under braking. 

Section 4R- Unless the car about to be lapped has sufficient pace to avoid being lapped, The car should be prepared to let the lapping car through safely and swiftly with minimal consequence such as lost delta.

Section 4S- When Drivers have an AI jump start, they must return to their Grid starting position by the end of lap 1, failure to do so will result in disqualfication from the Grand Prix, this is to also be applied for when the Driver jumps the start.

Section 5:Safety Car Restart

Section 5A- Driver leading the safety car inlap should set a consistent pace and not alter from that unsafely.

Section 5B- It is illegal to gain positions under the Safety Car or manipulate/take advantage of the starting sequence in any way.
- 5Bi - Drivers may only overtake after the start/finish line, this will result in a 5 second penalty and 2 penalty points.
- 5Bii - Drivers may not position their car alongside another car, also resulting in a 5 second penalty and 1 penalty point.
- 5Biii - For every place gained illegally the driver will be awarded a 5 second penalty unless the place is given back within 1 lap.
- 5Biv - Illegal overtaking also includes gaining positions through desync.

Section 5C- Any contact made under safety car whereby a driver obtains damage or consequently loses control of the car, will result in a 10 second penalty and 3 penalty points.

Section 6:FIA Submissions

Section 6A- Incidents will be judged by the FIA for each respective tier consisting off 3 or 4 members

Section 6B- FIA members may not pass biased judgements on inter-team incidents or any other incident.

Section 6C- Video footage provided to the FIA must be clear and show said incident.

Section 6D- Incident must be reported before the deadline for each respective tier or will not be considered unless opened by an owner or admin acting on behalf of an owner.

Section 6E- Reports must follow the template otherwise will be deleted and not considered.

Section 6F- Incidents submissions will be dismissed if a driver submits a incident that the driver isnt involved in. The exception on the rule is if the incident is a clear rule break. For example, A clear corner cut to gain a advantage and crossing the solid line of the pit entry/exit.

Section 7:Punishment Systems

Section 7A- Penalty point system:
6 Penalty Points: Quallification ban.
8 Penalty Points: Race Ban.
Penalty Points are then reset for the Driver from here and if they are to recieve any further penalty Points they are to be investigated by the owners.
Penalty points reset each season.

Section 8:Restart Procedure (Red Flags)

When restarting a lobby when necessary due to server issues, cheaters in the lobby or other reasons that the commentators/owners may see fit; follow the circumstances that happen when a Red Flag occurs.

Section 8A - Start of the race Red Flag. When there is a Red Flag during the start of a race, up to 25% race distance (laps 1-5 for example), the starting grid will be reset in the order of Qualifying/Sprint race results. The exceptions are by:
When a driver got a Drive Trough Penalty before the Red Flag. It will result in a back of the grid start;
When the driver in question has a unforced time penalty. The driver in question will be accordingly put back in the correct grid place.

Section 8B- 25% Race distance Red Flag. When there is a Red Flag when the Race has reached inbetween laps 5 and 8, the following options will be followed depending on the circumstance. There will be a 35% race to complete the GP. Starting grid will be decided 1 lap before the Red Flag has been waved. For example, when the Red Flag has been waved at Lap 7, the grid order will be from Lap 6; If decided by the Owners, the race may be voided and no points will be given.

Section 8C- 50% Race distance Red Flag. When there is a Red Flag when the Race has reached halfway through, the following options will be followed depending on the circumstance. There will be a 25% race to complete the GP. Starting grid will be decided 1 lap before the Red Flag has been waved. For example, when the Red Flag has been waved at Lap 16, the grid order be from Lap 15; When decided by the Owners, the race will be voided and half points will be given by the Top 10 finishers.

Section 8D- End of the Race Red Flag. When there is 75% Race distance has been covered, the Race will be voided and full points will be awarded to the drivers that finished in the top 10 positions.

Section 8E- Repeating Red Flags. When the lobby needs to be restarted on a repeating base, after 3 restarts any incomplete races will be voided. For example, when the Sprint Race in a Sprint Race Weekend is completed the Top 8 Finishers will be awarded Points that are normally given in Sprint Races. Any other cases the given Results will be decided by the Owners.

Section 8F- The Owners of the league are within their right to completely void a race due to poor standards or technical bugs. A Race voided in its entirity also means that points will not be awarded and Engine Penalties will have to be served at a different circuit.

Section 9:Engine Penalties

Section 9A- Engine Penalties have to be taken between Round 3 and Round 10 of season 10 in shape of a Quallification ban

Section 9B- Drivers who failed to serve a engine penalty within Round 3 till Round 10 will be Disqualified from the remaining rounds of the Season.

Section 9C- Each Full Time drivers have to take 1 Engine Penalty during a season.

Section 9D- Each F1 Team has to take 2 Engine Penalties in both Tiers during a season. That Engine Penalty being taken by all Full Time drivers each F1 has im their team. (See section 10C)

Section 9E- If a Full Time driver is replaced by another Full Time driver but the leaving Full Time driver has already served his Engine Penalty, the replacing Full Time driver doesn’t have to serve his Engine Penalty.

Section 10:Post-Season Promotions

Section 10 is in place to prevent unbalanced grids within the Tier 2 Championship and limit the possibilty of a driver dominating the competition. Promotions are when the driver is forced to compete exclusively within Tier 1 due to their level of skill.

Section 10A: The Tier 2 Champion is not permitted to retain Tier 2 classification if there is not ample competition to support a fair and fun Championship.

Section 10Bi: Drivers will be reviewed post-season and analysed on driver development by the Owners to determine whether their progression will warrant a promotion.
Section 10Bii: Similarly, drivers in Tier 1 will also be analysed to determine if the skill they present warrants a relegation to Tier 2.

Section 10C: Automatic promotions may occur if drivers meet any of the following requirements:
- 10Ci - The driver in question obtains a 60%+ win rate of races finished.
- 10Cii - The driver in question obtains a 100% podium rate of races finished.
- 10Ciii - The driver in question obtains over 250 points within the season.
